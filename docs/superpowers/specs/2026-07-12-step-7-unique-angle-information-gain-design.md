# Step 7 Unique Angle And Information Gain Design

## Purpose And Position

Step 7 is the hard synthesis gate after Step 6 Topic Research Bank and before Step 8 content briefing, Step 9 structure, final copy, images, page packet, commit, deploy, or publishing.

It answers: why does this page deserve to compete, and what visible value will it provide that existing results do not provide equivalently?

Step 7 performs no new research. It synthesizes the frozen Step 5 competitor analysis, Step 6 topic research, and Step 2 satisfaction requirements. Missing competitor understanding returns to Step 5. Missing topical evidence returns to Step 6.

## Artifacts And Hashes

Every page creates:

- `unique-angle-information-gain.json`: authoritative machine-validated contract.
- `unique-angle-information-gain.md`: human-readable review and debugging view.
- `uniqueAngleHash`: frozen contract hash carried by every downstream artifact.

`uniqueAngleInformationGain` must carry `step0BContractHash`, `pageJobHash`, `searchIntentHash`, `pageFormatHash`, `nextActionHash`, `serpCompetitorHash`, `topicResearchHash`, and `uniqueAngleHash`.

Hash mismatches return to the owner step. Step 7 cannot modify an upstream contract. If an angle, asset, baseline, area to exceed, reason-to-compete statement, or delivery requirement changes after freezing, regenerate and revalidate Step 7.

## Improvement Angles

Require exactly one `primaryImprovementAngle` and 1-3 `supportingImprovementAngles`. The primary angle controls differentiation; supporting angles strengthen specific needs without competing for page identity.

Allowed angle types are `clarity`, `depth`, `practicality`, `example_led`, `framework_led`, `freshness`, `trust`, `specificity`, `decision_support`, `local_market_fit`, `safety_boundary`, `troubleshooting`, `original_synthesis`, and `other`.

`other` requires `whyExistingTypesDoNotFit`. Every angle requires a page-specific statement that identifies what becomes better, for whom, compared with what, using which evidence, and how the improvement becomes visible. Generic labels such as "more comprehensive", "better researched", or "more useful" fail.

## Information-Gain Assets

Every page requires:

- One primary asset that is mandatory and visibly delivered.
- One mandatory supporting asset that is visibly delivered.
- One optional supporting asset controlled by `includeWhen`, `omitWhen`, and `minimumReaderValue`.

Allowed asset types include `decision_matrix`, `step_by_step_workflow`, `troubleshooting_map`, `comparison_table`, `fit_check_framework`, `mistake_to_fix_map`, `example_bank`, `before_after_scenario_set`, `safety_boundary_box`, `local_market_fit_guide`, `myth_vs_reality_table`, `cost_or_effort_breakdown`, `timeline_or_sequence_map`, `diagnostic_question_set`, `template_or_script_preview`, `original_synthesis_block`, and `other`.

`other` requires `whyExistingTypesDoNotFit`.

Step 7 defines a functional specification only. Each asset needs purpose, inputs, output, visible format, reader use, `readerActionValue`, evidence mapping, intent mapping, satisfaction-condition mapping, scope boundaries, safety boundaries, `accessibleContentFallback`, and delivery requirements. Step 8 defines detailed content and Step 9 determines exact placement.

Every asset, including an interactive, visual, or image-enhanced asset, must have a complete text, table, checklist, or flow fallback that preserves its reader value in final copy. Enhanced media cannot be the only delivery.

Step 8 or Step 9 may include or omit the optional asset according to Step 7's frozen conditions. The decision must be evidence-linked. Omission under approved conditions does not break `uniqueAngleHash`; any other omission returns to Step 7.

## Evidence Minimums

The primary asset requires at least 2 Step 5 references, at least 3 Step 6 evidence items from at least 2 credible sources, at least one top intent dimension, and at least one Step 2 satisfaction condition.

The mandatory supporting asset requires at least 2 Step 6 evidence items plus either Step 5 evidence or audience/search-language evidence.

The optional supporting asset requires at least one strong source and explicit reader value.

For objections, FAQs, mistakes, and troubleshooting assets, at least one audience-language reference is mandatory when available. Safety, medical, skincare, finance, legal, or other sensitive logic requires appropriate authoritative Step 6 support.

## Information-Gain Qualification

The primary asset must pass the evidence + synthesis + action test. It must combine validated evidence into a page-specific interpretation, decision, workflow, comparison, or troubleshooting outcome that changes what the reader can understand or do without inventing facts.

Two `superiorityPath` values are allowed:

- `empty_gap`: fill a proven useful gap that competitors do not address equivalently.
- `material_improvement`: improve an existing component on decision usefulness, local fit, safety, clarity, evidence, or usability.

Each superiority item must name the competitor evidence, current limitation, proposed functional improvement, improved dimension, final verification method, and reader impact. Mere absence, attractive formatting, ordinary SERP coverage, or cosmetic component changes do not qualify.

## Baseline And Areas To Exceed

`baselineToMatch` requires at least 2 evidence-linked baseline requirements. Baseline expectations are credibility requirements, not information gain.

`areasToExceed` requires 5-7 functionally distinct improvements, including the primary angle, primary asset, mandatory supporting asset, and at least two additional section-level improvements. More than 7 requires `exceedLimitReason`, Step 2 depth support, Step 5 competitive-bar evidence, and proof that scope does not drift.

Each item needs `requirement`, `reason`, `evidenceRefs`, `mappedIntentDimension`, `pageImpact`, and `deliveryProofRequired`. Items must serve different reader needs or competitive weaknesses rather than restating the same asset.

Collectively, the improvements must demonstrate `multiSurfaceEvidenceCoverage` from primary SERP competitors, credible Step 6 research, at least one secondary-query or long-tail insight, and at least one audience/search-language insight when available. Sensitive claims additionally require authoritative evidence.

## Explicit Routing

Every relevant Step 5 gap must be routed as `address_in_primary_angle`, `address_in_supporting_asset`, `briefly_support`, `separate_page_candidate`, `reject_as_irrelevant`, or `return_to_step5`.

Every relevant Step 6 opportunity, especially `mustCarryForward` and page-use items, must be routed as `use_in_primary_angle`, `use_in_primary_asset`, `use_in_supporting_asset`, `support_normal_content`, `route_to_later_step`, `separate_page_candidate`, `reject_with_reason`, or `return_to_step6`.

Every routing item requires `reason`, `evidenceRefs`, `readerValue`, `pageImpact`, and `deliveryProofRequired`. No finding may silently disappear or enter scope.

## Required Statements

`informationGainStatement`, `differentiationStatement`, and `whyThisPageDeservesToCompeteStatement` are mandatory.

The reason-to-compete statement must name the main intent win, Step 2 satisfaction condition, competitor limitation, research-backed differentiation, primary visible asset, and practical reader outcome. Generic superiority claims fail.

## Format, Safety, Brand, And Originality

`formatCompatibilityCheck` is a hard gate. Mandatory assets must fit the frozen Step 3 page type and internal format. Minor presentation adaptation is allowed; a real format conflict returns to Step 3.

`safetyAndBrandFitCheck` must prove that the angle and assets are reader-first, within demonstrated brand expertise, compatible with Step 1 risk boundaries and Step 6 safety evidence, non-diagnostic/non-deceptive when relevant, and not primarily conversion devices.

`originalityContract` prohibits copied competitor wording, headings, examples, tables, frameworks, decision logic, section sequences, and close paraphrases. Competitor claims are not factual evidence without Step 6 validation. Page-specific imitation warnings remain owned by Step 5.

## Batch And Historical Uniqueness

`uniqueAngleUniquenessCheck` compares the current page with current-batch and accessible historical Step 7 artifacts. It checks angles, asset purpose, reader outcome, evidence mapping, decision logic, and functional inputs/outputs.

Reusing an asset type is allowed. Reusing substantially the same reasoning, rows, branches, evidence pattern, or reader outcome is blocked and repaired. Inaccessible historical artifacts may produce `pass_with_warnings`; current-batch uniqueness is mandatory.

Every page creates its own Step 7 artifact pair. Shared or copied Step 7 artifacts are forbidden unless every upstream hash is identical, which should almost never happen.

## Must Carry Forward And Delivery Proof

`mustCarryForward` contains concise references to the primary and supporting angles, primary and mandatory supporting assets, optional-asset conditions, `baselineToMatch`, `areasToExceed`, reason-to-compete statement, originality boundaries, evidence requirements, delivery requirements, and `uniqueAngleHash`.

Final copy or page-packet QA must create `informationGainDeliveryProof` mapping visible final-copy or page-packet references to the primary angle, primary asset, mandatory supporting asset, every `areasToExceed` item, required baseline items, and reason-to-compete statement. The optional asset needs visible delivery or an evidence-linked omission reason. Missing required proof blocks publishing and triggers repair.

## Boundary Contract

`step7OutputMustNotContain` prohibits final outline, H1/H2/H3 headings, section order, detailed asset rows/branches/content, final prose, final CTA wording, metadata, image prompts, citation-display strategy, new research, and unsupported claims.

Step 7 chooses why and how the page will be distinct. Step 8 briefs the content. Step 9 builds the structure.

## Completeness Checklist

`step7CompletenessChecklist` must verify upstream hashes, angle selection, Step 5 gap routing, Step 6 opportunity routing, asset specifications, evidence minimums, top-intent mapping, satisfaction-condition mapping, information-gain qualification, baseline minimums, 5-7 distinct areas to exceed, multi-surface evidence, format compatibility, safety/brand fit, originality, batch/history uniqueness, reason-to-compete statement, `mustCarryForward`, delivery requirements, boundary compliance, and no independent research.

False values trigger repair, warning, fail, or owner-step routing according to criticality. A present but empty or generic field does not count as complete.

## Verdict, Repairs, And Routing

`uniqueAngleVerdict.status` may be `pass`, `pass_with_warnings`, `fail`, or `ask_user`. `confidence` may be `low`, `medium`, or `high`. No numeric scorecard is allowed.

`pass_with_warnings` is limited to inaccessible historical uniqueness data, optional-asset omission risk, genuinely unavailable audience-language evidence, or a non-critical source-diversity limitation. Primary angle, mandatory assets, evidence, intent, safety, format, current-batch uniqueness, and five-improvement requirements are hard blockers.

Step 7 may auto-repair weak fields up to 2 times: missing reasons, weak statements, incomplete routing, missing evidence refs, vague reader value, missing asset boundaries, weak delivery requirements, or an incomplete carry-forward block.

Do not auto-repair no defensible primary angle, no mandatory asset, insufficient Step 5/6 evidence, scope or intent conflict, unsafe differentiation, Step 3 incompatibility, copied competitor logic, or substantial batch/historical duplication. Route to the owner step, `ask_user`, or `skip_page` in batch.

Allowed actions are `continue_to_step8`, `return_to_step6`, `return_to_step5`, `return_to_step4`, `return_to_step3`, `return_to_step2`, `return_to_step1`, `return_to_0B`, `ask_user`, and `skip_page`.

Unresolved single-page runs normally use `ask_user`. Unresolved batch pages record the exact failure and use `skip_page` so the batch can continue.
